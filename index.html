Here's a time line of Ferdinand Marcos life:
  • 1965 - Ferdinand Marcos gets elected as the first President of the Philippines on November 1965, and officially took office as President in December 30, 1965
  • 1969 - Ferdinand Marcos suspiciously becomes the first man in the Philippines to get re-elected as President. This event causes an uproar to the citizens
  • 1970 - Students raged and attempted to storm into the Malaca√±ang Palace. When the front gate opened, there was an eerie silences, that gunshots happened. 4 students were injured, and many were injured. It was a riot against the Police.

  • 1976 - Imelda Marcos successfully gets an agreement with Libya to stop supporting Nur Misuari and the Moro National Liberation Front who are fighting against Marcos and his army. Carmelo Z.   • 1978 - After Marcos held the elections, the political party that was formed by Ninoy Aquino held 21 candidates for the Metro Manila Area.
  • 1980 - Ninoy Aquino gets permission from President Marcos to travel to the US in order to get heart surgery (he had a heart attack). He succeeded his heart surgery at May 13, 1980
  • 1981 - Ferdinand Marcos wins another term as he ran against weak candidates in this election. He won more than 18 million votes
  • 1983 - On August 21, 1983, Ninoy Aquino arrives at the Philippines' National Airport. As soon as he gets off, he gets shot, and dies. This makes the whole nation sad, but also gives birth of the People Power Revolution.
  • 1985 - Ferdinand Marcos calls for snap elections because he finds out that The US are starting to break their trust and their relationship with him and the nation because of the communists and he thinks the country is leaving democracy
  • 1986 - On February 25, 1986, Ferdinand Marcos flees to Guam after almost all of the people in the Philippines opposed him and wanted him to resign. Even his own military forces joined the opposing citizens. He had no choice but to flee. This became a huge day in the Philippines' History since it was the first time People Power Revolution (No violence) took real action and succeeded.
  • 1986 - Fidel Ramos and Juan Ponce Enrile left Marcos' army in order to support the people opposing Marcos. This was a really hard move for Marcos especially since Marcos was his cousin. Marcos immediately threatened them with attacks.
  • 1989 - Ferdinand Marcos dies in Hawaii. The main cause of his death was the rare and deadly Lupus, which was a disease.
